Group Programs

Healing through community, mindfulness, embodied movement, and shared experience.

Beyond individual and couples therapy, we offer specialized group programs rooted in evidence-based practice and designed to support different stages of healing and growth. These programs create community, reduce isolation, and offer unique benefits that traditional therapy may not provide.

Therapeutic Yoga

Mindful movement and breath work for stress relief, embodied healing, and wellness.

About This Program

Therapeutic yoga combines gentle movement, breathwork, and mindfulness to support mental health and emotional wellbeing. Unlike fitness yoga, our therapeutic approach emphasizes feeling safe in your body, releasing tension held by trauma or stress, and building awareness of mind-body connection.

What to Expect

Our instructors create a welcoming, non-judgmental space where everyone is invited to listen to their body and modify poses as needed. Sessions include:

  • Gentle stretching and alignment-based movement
  • Pranayama (breathwork) for nervous system regulation
  • Grounding and somatic awareness practices
  • Guided relaxation and savasana

Who Benefits

  • People managing stress, anxiety, or trauma
  • Those seeking to reconnect with their body
  • Anyone wanting to supplement individual therapy with embodied practices
  • People interested in yoga but preferring a mental health focus

Schedule & Registration

Groups typically meet weekly for 60-75 minutes. Beginner and continuing levels available. Contact us for current schedule and registration information.

Meditation & Mindfulness

MBSR-based practices for resilience, presence, and compassion.

About This Program

Our mindfulness programs are rooted in Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) and other evidence-based meditation practices. We teach practical skills for managing stress, anxiety, chronic pain, and difficult emotions through cultivating present-moment awareness and self-compassion.

What to Expect

Each session includes:

  • Guided sitting and walking meditation
  • Body scan practices for somatic awareness
  • Informal mindfulness in daily life
  • Discussion of mindfulness principles and applications
  • Loving-kindness and self-compassion practices

Research & Benefits

Scientific research shows mindfulness practices reduce anxiety, depression, stress reactivity, and chronic pain while improving focus, emotional regulation, and overall wellbeing. Regular practice is key—we encourage attendance at 6-8 week or 8-week programs.

Different Programs Offered

  • Drop-in Meditation Group: Weekly open meditation session for experienced and beginning practitioners
  • 8-Week MBSR-Based Program: Structured course with home practice
  • Workplace Mindfulness: Available for organizations interested in staff wellness programs

Schedule & Registration

Drop-in groups meet weekly; structured programs typically run 8 weeks. Contact us for current offerings.

Infant Massage Instruction

Teaching parents to use therapeutic touch to bond with and soothe their baby.

About This Program

Infant massage is a gentle, nurturing practice that strengthens parent-baby bonding while supporting the baby's development, digestion, sleep, and overall health. We teach parents and caregivers evidence-based techniques in a supportive group setting.

What to Expect

Our instructor-led sessions include:

  • Demonstration of safe, effective massage techniques
  • Opportunity to practice with your own baby
  • Discussion of how massage supports development and attachment
  • Guidance on reading baby's cues and responsiveness
  • Peer support with other parents

Who Should Attend

  • New parents wanting to deepen bonding with their baby
  • Parents interested in natural soothing and sleep support
  • Caregivers (grandparents, nannies, etc.) seeking connection techniques
  • Parents interested in infant development and attachment science

Age & Timing

Best for babies 2 weeks to 12 months old. Can begin as early as 2 weeks postpartum.

Schedule & Registration

Classes typically run for 4-6 weeks and meet weekly. Contact us for current schedule and pricing.

Prenatal & Postnatal Therapeutic Groups

Support and connection during pregnancy, birth preparation, and the fourth trimester.

About These Programs

We offer specialized groups for expectant parents and new families. These groups combine psychoeducation, peer support, and therapeutic guidance for one of life's most transformative periods. Research shows group support improves outcomes for anxiety, depression, bonding, and adjustment.

Prenatal Groups

For pregnant individuals and their partners, offered in trimesters or all-pregnancy groups. Topics include:

  • Anxiety and worry during pregnancy
  • Birth preparation and coping skills
  • Identity transition to parenthood
  • Partner relationship changes and support
  • Managing previous trauma, loss, or grief

Postnatal & New Parent Groups

For parents in the first year postpartum, addressing:

  • Postpartum mood and anxiety disorders (screening, support, treatment)
  • Adjustment to new parenthood and identity shifts
  • Infant sleep, feeding, and developmental concerns
  • Partner relationships and communication postpartum
  • Body image and postpartum recovery
  • Parenting confidence and bonding

Who Should Attend

  • Pregnant individuals (all pregnancy types)
  • Partners and co-parents
  • First-time parents
  • Parents navigating multiple births, adoption, or blended families
  • Anyone seeking connection and support during perinatal period

Schedule & Registration

Groups typically meet weekly or bi-weekly for 8-12 weeks. Contact us for current schedule and to register.

Professional CEU Events

Continuing education for mental health professionals, clinicians, and related fields.

About Our CEU Programs

We host and co-host continuing education workshops, seminars, and trainings for licensed mental health professionals and related practitioners. Our programs combine cutting-edge research with practical clinical applications and promote an ethical, evidence-based approach to practice.

Past & Upcoming Topics (Examples)

  • Trauma-Informed Care and EMDR applications
  • Working with Complex PTSD and attachment
  • DBT fundamentals and skills coaching
  • Substance use treatment and harm reduction
  • Telehealth ethics and best practices
  • Ethical practice in cooperative and worker-owned organizations
  • Perinatal mental health screening and intervention
  • Neurodiversity-affirming therapy

Who Attends

  • LCSW, LMFT, LPC, LADC, and other licensed clinicians
  • Psychologists and psychiatrists
  • Nurse practitioners and clinical nurses
  • Counselors, coaches, and related professionals seeking continued learning

CEU Accreditation

Events are offered through our partnerships with accredited providers. Specifics vary by event. Contact us for current CEU offerings and registration.

Professional Networking

Our CEU events also serve as opportunities to connect with other local practitioners, share referrals, and strengthen the mental health community in Northwest Connecticut.

Experiential & Somatic Groups

Body-centered, creative, and experiential approaches to healing.

About This Program

We offer various experiential and somatic groups that go beyond traditional talk therapy. These modalities recognize that trauma, stress, and emotion are stored in the body and that healing can happen through movement, creative expression, and somatic awareness.

Offered Modalities (May Include)

  • Somatic Experiencing (SE) Groups: Body-based approach to processing and releasing trauma
  • Movement & Dance Therapy: Expressive movement for emotional processing and joy
  • Art & Creative Expression Groups: Visual art, writing, and creative work for self-exploration
  • Breathwork & Grounding Groups: Nervous system regulation through breath and embodiment
  • Wilderness & Nature-Based Groups: Healing through outdoor experience and natural settings (seasonal)

Who Benefits

  • People with trauma or PTSD who want body-centered work
  • Those who find talk therapy alone insufficient
  • Creative individuals seeking expressive outlets
  • People interested in embodied, holistic approaches to healing

Schedule & Registration

Offerings vary seasonally. Contact us to inquire about current experiential and somatic groups.
