Medication Management & Psychiatry

Psychiatric evaluation and expert medication optimization by our PMHNP-BC—the cornerstone of comprehensive mental health care.

Psychiatric Evaluation & Diagnosis

Our Board-Certified Psychiatric Nurse Practitioner (PMHNP-BC) conducts thorough psychiatric assessments to understand your unique history, current symptoms, family psychiatric history, medication response patterns, and overall health. Initial evaluations typically take 60-90 minutes and include a comprehensive review of your symptoms, triggers, past treatments, lifestyle factors, and goals for care.

Conditions We Treat with Medication

  • Depression & Mood Disorders: Major depression, persistent depressive disorder, bipolar disorder, cyclothymia
  • Anxiety Disorders: Generalized anxiety disorder, panic disorder, social anxiety disorder, specific phobias, agoraphobia
  • ADHD: Attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder across the lifespan—children, adolescents, and adults
  • Trauma-Related Disorders: PTSD, acute stress disorder, complex trauma
  • OCD & Related Disorders: Obsessive-compulsive disorder, body-focused repetitive behaviors, hoarding
  • Substance Use Disorders: Alcohol and opioid use disorders with medication-assisted treatment
  • Sleep Disorders: Insomnia, hypersomnia, and medication-related sleep disturbances
  • Personality Patterns & Chronic Dysregulation: Borderline, avoidant, and other personality-related presentations

Medications We Prescribe

  • Antidepressants: SSRIs, SNRIs, tricyclics, and other evidence-based classes
  • Anti-Anxiety Medications: Non-benzodiazepine anxiolytics and short-term benzodiazepine management
  • Mood Stabilizers: Lithium, anticonvulsants, and other mood-regulating agents
  • Stimulants & ADHD Medications: Amphetamines, methylphenidate, and non-stimulant alternatives (atomoxetine, guanfacine)
  • Antipsychotics: First and second-generation agents for psychotic and mood disorders
  • Controlled Substance Management: Responsible prescribing of schedule II-IV medications with ongoing monitoring

Medication Optimization & Monitoring

Finding the right medication at the right dose is an art and a science. We use a thoughtful, collaborative approach: starting with careful assessment of your individual neurobiology, trying medications at effective doses, monitoring side effects closely, adjusting as needed, and involving you in every decision. Regular follow-up appointments (typically every 4-8 weeks) ensure your medication continues to work well and that any side effects are managed.

What We Monitor: Symptom improvement, side effect profile, lab values (when indicated), medication interactions, and your overall wellbeing and functioning. We use evidence-based rating scales and your subjective experience to guide adjustments.

Collaborative Care Model

Medication works best when combined with therapy, lifestyle changes, and community support. Our PMHNP-BC works closely with your individual therapist (if you have one) to coordinate care, share treatment goals, and ensure you're receiving integrated support. We communicate regularly with your other providers (primary care, specialists, therapists) when clinically appropriate and with your consent.

Controlled Substance Responsibility

We take controlled substance management seriously. For stimulants (ADHD medications), benzodiazepines, and other schedule II-IV substances, we maintain careful monitoring, conduct regular risk assessments, and follow state and federal guidelines. We use state prescription drug monitoring programs (PDMP) to prevent misuse and diversion.

Future Interventional Treatments

We are committed to staying at the forefront of psychiatric care. Interventional treatments such as Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS) for treatment-resistant depression and Spravato (esketamine) for severe depression are under evaluation. Contact us for information about availability and eligibility.

What to Expect at Your First Appointment

  • 60-90 minute comprehensive evaluation covering psychiatric history, family history, current medications, medical history, and symptoms
  • Diagnostic assessment to determine what's happening and what treatment might help
  • Collaborative discussion about medication options, risks, benefits, and alternatives
  • Treatment plan developed together, including medication recommendations if appropriate
  • Baseline labs if needed for safe prescribing

Follow-Up Appointments

After your initial evaluation, follow-up medication management appointments typically last 30-45 minutes and are scheduled every 4-8 weeks, depending on your needs. More frequent appointments may be recommended when starting new medication or making major adjustments.

Individual Therapy

Personalized, evidence-based therapy tailored to your unique needs—from anxiety and depression to trauma and life transitions. Available via Telehealth

Conditions We Treat

  • Depression: Major depression, persistent depressive disorder, seasonal depression, grief and loss
  • Anxiety Disorders: Generalized anxiety disorder, panic disorder, social anxiety, specific phobias, agoraphobia
  • PTSD & Complex Trauma: Single-incident trauma, complex trauma, childhood abuse, medical trauma, PTSD
  • ADHD & Neurodivergence: ADHD management, autism-affirming therapy, sensory processing support
  • OCD & Related Disorders: Obsessive-compulsive disorder, body-focused repetitive behaviors, health anxiety
  • Life Transitions: Career changes, relationship loss, identity exploration, life purpose and meaning
  • Personality Patterns: Relationship patterns, interpersonal challenges, self-esteem and identity issues

Evidence-Based Modalities We Use

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) helps you identify thought patterns that may be contributing to distress and develop new, more helpful ways of thinking and behaving.

Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) combines cognitive-behavioral techniques with acceptance strategies, particularly helpful for managing intense emotions, self-harm urges, and relationship challenges.

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) focuses on accepting difficult thoughts and feelings while committing to actions aligned with your values.

Psychodynamic Therapy explores how past experiences and unconscious patterns influence current struggles, building insight and emotional depth.

Somatic & Body-Based Approaches recognize that trauma and emotion are held in the body; we use techniques like sensorimotor psychotherapy, yoga, and breathwork to support healing.

Your Therapist's Approach

Your therapist will work collaboratively with you to understand what has brought you to therapy and to develop a treatment plan that honors your values, goals, and lived experience. We practice cultural humility, trauma-informed care, and celebrate neurodiversity. Every person is met with respect, without judgment.

EMDR: Specialized Trauma Treatment

What Is EMDR? Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR) is an evidence-based therapy specifically designed for trauma processing. Waypoint Cooperative features EMDR-trained clinicians who specialize in this powerful modality.

How EMDR Works: EMDR uses a structured 8-phase protocol that engages your brain's natural healing mechanisms through bilateral stimulation (eye movements, alternating tapping, or auditory cues). While recalling a traumatic memory, you simultaneously engage in bilateral stimulation, which allows your brain to process the trauma and reduce its emotional charge. Unlike traditional talk therapy, EMDR doesn't require you to recount every detail of trauma—instead, your mind naturally processes and integrates the memory.

The 8-Phase EMDR Protocol:

  1. History & Treatment Planning: Building your history and understanding the trauma to be processed
  2. Preparation: Teaching you coping skills and establishing safety before trauma processing
  3. Assessment: Identifying the specific image, belief, emotion, and body sensation related to the trauma
  4. Desensitization: Using bilateral stimulation while thinking of the traumatic memory to process and reduce emotional intensity
  5. Installation: Strengthening a positive belief to replace the trauma-related belief
  6. Body Scan: Checking for residual trauma-related tension in the body and processing as needed
  7. Closure: Ensuring emotional safety between sessions
  8. Re-evaluation: Assessing progress and planning ongoing treatment

Who Benefits from EMDR: EMDR is effective for individuals with PTSD, single-incident trauma, childhood abuse, complex trauma, anxiety disorders, panic disorder, phobias, grief, and other trauma-related conditions. It's safe and effective across age ranges and can be adapted for children and adolescents.

Treatment Length: The duration varies based on your needs. Simple, single-incident trauma may respond well to brief, focused EMDR (6-12 sessions), while complex trauma may require longer-term treatment (3-12 months or more). You'll develop a plan with your therapist based on your history and goals.

First Session & What to Expect

Your first session (typically 50-60 minutes) will focus on getting to know you, understanding your concerns, and discussing what therapy might look like. We'll talk about your history, current challenges, previous therapy experience, and goals for treatment. You'll learn about different therapy approaches and decide together which might be the best fit. If EMDR is appropriate, we'll discuss its specific process and benefits.

Ongoing Therapy

Most clients attend weekly 50-minute sessions. Frequency may adjust based on your needs—some find benefit in twice-weekly sessions during difficult periods, while others transition to biweekly or monthly sessions as they progress. Treatment length varies widely; some work in therapy for a focused 3-6 months, while others engage in longer-term therapeutic relationships for years.

Telehealth Options

Individual therapy is available via secure video telehealth. This is ideal if you have transportation challenges, live in a rural area, or prefer the convenience of in-home sessions. All privacy and clinical standards remain the same as in-person therapy.

Couples & Family Therapy

Heal relationships, improve communication, and build healthier family systems. Available via Telehealth

Couples Therapy

Relationships go through difficult periods—conflict, disconnection, infidelity, communication breakdown, major life transitions. We work with couples who are motivated to understand each other better and build a stronger, more intimate connection.

What We Address: Unresolved conflict, emotional disconnection, communication patterns, infidelity and trust issues, intimacy and sexuality concerns, major transitions (parenthood, career changes, loss), parenting as a couple, and building shared values and goals.

Our Approach: We use research-based methods including Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT), which helps you understand the emotional needs beneath conflict; the Gottman Method, which focuses on building friendship and intimacy; and Imago Relationship Therapy, which helps you see your partner with fresh eyes. We help you understand each other's attachment patterns, develop practical communication tools, build emotional safety, and reconnect as a team.

Family Systems Work

Family therapy helps parents and children communicate better, resolve conflict, and build stronger relationships. We work with parents and one child, entire families, adult siblings, or multigenerational systems.

Common Reasons for Family Therapy: Parenting challenges, sibling conflict, blended family adjustment, grief and loss, intergenerational patterns, supporting a family member's mental health recovery, preparing for major family changes (divorce, remarriage, relocation).

Co-Parenting Support

Whether separated, divorced, or together, we help parents build healthy co-parenting relationships and raise children with intention. We focus on trauma-informed parenting, managing high-conflict dynamics, reducing children's exposure to conflict, and supporting children through transitions.

Who Can Participate

Couples therapy works best when both partners are willing to engage with openness and commitment. Family therapy may include parents and children, adult siblings, step-relations, or multigenerational groups depending on the presenting issues.

First Session & What to Expect

In your first session (60-90 minutes), we'll meet with all participants and learn about your relationships, what has brought you to therapy, and what you hope to achieve. We'll discuss how therapy works and what to expect going forward.

Ongoing Sessions

Most couples and families attend weekly sessions of 50-60 minutes. Treatment length varies—some work together for 3-6 months on a specific issue, while others engage in longer-term therapy to deepen relationships over time.

Substance Use & Dual Diagnosis Treatment

Compassionate, evidence-based care for addiction and substance use disorders, with expert treatment of co-occurring mental health conditions. Available via Telehealth

Conditions We Treat

  • Alcohol Use Disorder: Mild to severe, all stages of recovery from active use to long-term sobriety
  • Drug Use Disorders: Opioids, stimulants (methamphetamine, cocaine), cannabis, benzodiazepines, hallucinogens, and other substances
  • Dual Diagnosis (Substance Use + Mental Health): The complex interplay of addiction and depression, anxiety, trauma, ADHD, bipolar disorder, personality disorders, or other mental health conditions
  • Behavioral Addictions: Gambling, food, sex, internet/gaming use patterns, shopping

Our Philosophy

We believe recovery is possible for everyone. People can make meaningful changes when they're ready and when they have support. We meet you where you are—whether you're contemplating change, actively struggling with use, in early recovery, or working to maintain long-term sobriety. We believe that treating underlying mental health issues is essential for lasting recovery and that addressing the whole person—mind, body, relationships, and values—leads to the deepest healing.

Evidence-Based Treatment Approaches

Motivational Interviewing (MI) helps you explore ambivalence about change and find your own intrinsic motivation for recovery.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) for Substance Use identifies triggers and thought patterns that maintain use, and develops new coping skills and behavioral strategies.

Relapse Prevention Planning prepares you for high-risk situations and builds a toolkit of strategies to maintain recovery.

12-Step Integration honors the value of 12-step programs while integrating them with evidence-based clinical care. We support your participation in AA, NA, SMART Recovery, or other mutual-aid programs.

Addressing Trauma Many people with substance use disorders have experienced trauma. We offer trauma-informed care and can provide specialized treatments like EMDR when appropriate.

Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)

In collaboration with our PMHNP-BC, we offer medication-assisted treatment for opioid and alcohol use disorders. Medications such as buprenorphine, naltrexone, and acamprosate can reduce cravings, block euphoric effects, and restore brain chemistry balance. MAT is particularly effective when combined with counseling and behavioral therapies.

Dual Diagnosis Expertise

Many people struggling with substance use also have depression, anxiety, PTSD, ADHD, or other mental health conditions. These conditions often fuel each other—untreated depression or anxiety may drive substance use as self-medication, while substance use worsens mood and anxiety. We treat both conditions simultaneously, coordinating medication management with individual therapy to achieve the best outcomes.

Federal Confidentiality Protection

All substance use treatment records are protected under 42 CFR Part 2 federal confidentiality regulations, providing special protection beyond standard HIPAA. This means your treatment records cannot be disclosed to law enforcement or other third parties without your explicit written consent (with narrow exceptions).

Family Support

Addiction affects the whole family. We welcome family members into the therapeutic process when appropriate and offer family therapy, psychoeducation, and support groups for loved ones. We help families understand addiction, establish healthy boundaries, and heal relationships damaged by substance use.

First Session & What to Expect

Your initial assessment (60-90 minutes) includes a detailed substance use history, mental health assessment, medical history, and discussion of your goals for treatment. We'll talk openly about your relationship with substances, what's worked in the past, and what kind of support might help you now.

Treatment Planning

Based on your assessment, we'll develop a personalized treatment plan that may include individual therapy, medication-assisted treatment, group support, and/or family involvement. Most clients attend weekly sessions; some benefit from more frequent support, especially in early recovery.

Child & Adolescent Services

Specialized mental health care for children and teens at every developmental stage. Available via Telehealth

Conditions We Treat

  • Depression & Mood Changes: Major depression, persistent sadness, irritability, withdrawal, self-harm urges
  • Anxiety Disorders: Generalized anxiety, social anxiety, school refusal, separation anxiety, panic
  • ADHD: Inattention, hyperactivity, impulsivity; medication management for children and teens
  • Trauma & PTSD: Response to violence, abuse, accident, loss; EMDR and trauma-focused therapy
  • Behavioral & Conduct Issues: Defiance, aggression, oppositional behavior, school conflicts
  • Grief & Loss: Death of a loved one, parental divorce, moving, major life changes
  • Eating Disorders: Anorexia, bulimia, binge eating, restrictive eating, body image concerns
  • Self-Injury & Self-Harm: Cutting, burning, or other self-injurious behaviors
  • Substance Use: Experimentation, problematic use, or dependence in teens
  • Identity & Development: LGBTQ+ identity exploration, gender dysphoria, sexual development, peer relationships

Why Children & Teens Need Different Care

Young people face unique developmental challenges: navigating peer relationships, school stress, identity formation, body changes, family dynamics, and a rapidly changing social world (including social media). They're still developing the brain regions responsible for emotional regulation and decision-making. Our clinicians are trained in child and adolescent mental health and understand how to meet young people where they are developmentally.

Services Include

  • Individual Therapy: For depression, anxiety, trauma, grief, behavioral challenges, and life transitions
  • Medication Management: Psychiatric evaluation and medication for ADHD, depression, anxiety, bipolar disorder, and other conditions. Our PMHNP-BC specializes in pediatric psychopharmacology.
  • Family Therapy: When family dynamics are relevant to the teen's wellbeing; helps parents understand their child and improve communication
  • School Consultation & Educational Advocacy: Communication with teachers, development of 504 plans or IEPs, support for accommodations
  • Group Therapy & Peer Support: For adolescents to build social skills, connect with peers, and feel less alone
  • Parent Coaching: Helping parents understand their child's mental health, manage behavior, and support recovery

Parent Involvement & Consent

For Children Under 16: Parental involvement is part of standard care. We communicate with parents about general progress, treatment goals, and strategies you can use at home. With your permission, we may consult with school staff or other professionals involved in your child's care.

For Adolescents 16+: Under Connecticut law (CGS § 19a-14c), young people 16 and older may seek mental health services without parental consent. We respect adolescent autonomy and the importance of confidentiality during this developmental stage. At the same time, we recognize the value of parental support when appropriate. We work with each teen and family to find a balance that honors the teen's privacy while maintaining helpful parental communication about safety and progress.

First Session & What to Expect

We typically see children and adolescents with at least one parent present for the initial session. This allows us to understand the family's perspective and the teen to feel supported. Depending on the teen's age and preference, we may meet with parents separately to discuss confidentiality and treatment goals.

Ongoing Treatment

Most children and teens benefit from weekly 50-minute sessions. Parents often join selected sessions to learn strategies and stay informed. Treatment duration varies widely depending on the presenting concern—some young people work with us for 3-6 months on a specific issue, while others engage in longer-term therapy through adolescence.

Telehealth for Youth

Many adolescents prefer telehealth, especially if they're busy with school and activities or prefer the privacy of their own space. We can conduct therapy via secure video from your home.

Group Therapy

Healing through shared experience, peer support, and evidence-based group modalities. Some Available via Telehealth

Group Modalities We Offer

Process Groups are open-ended therapy groups where members share their experiences and receive feedback and support from peers and the therapist. These groups help you understand patterns in yourself and relationships, build social skills, and feel less alone in your struggles.

Psychoeducational Groups focus on building specific skills or knowledge—such as anxiety management, depression recovery, healthy relationships, emotional regulation, or stress management. They combine teaching with peer discussion and practice.

DBT Skills Groups teach Dialectical Behavior Therapy skills in mindfulness, distress tolerance, emotion regulation, and interpersonal effectiveness. These are particularly helpful for those managing intense emotions, self-harm urges, or personality-related challenges.

Support Groups connect people with shared experiences—grief support, recovery from addiction, parenting challenges, chronic illness, or other specific struggles.

Benefits of Group Therapy

Groups provide connection and reduce isolation, normalize your struggles, offer diverse perspectives and feedback from peers, help you practice new skills in relationships, and are often more affordable than individual therapy. Many people find the support of others who truly understand their experience to be profoundly healing.

For clients without insurance or preferring to self-pay, we offer competitive rates. Ask about our detailed self-pay fee schedule.
