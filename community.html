Community

Waypoint is more than a practice — we're a community resource. As a worker-owned cooperative, we're invested in the wellbeing of Northwest Connecticut.

The Cooperative Difference

Worker-Ownership Creates Stability

When clinicians are invested partners rather than employees, they stay. Reduced turnover means you build lasting relationships with your provider and benefit from continuity of care.

Profits Reinvested in Community

Our revenue doesn't flow to outside shareholders. Instead, we reinvest in sliding scale capacity, specialized programs, community training, and staff development—strengthening mental health resources across Northwest Connecticut.

Collaborative Decision-Making

Every clinician has a voice. This cooperative governance leads to better clinical decisions, more ethical practice, and genuine accountability to our clients and community.

Growing Community of Clinicians

We're actively seeking licensed mental health professionals interested in worker ownership. Join a practice where your wellbeing matters and your voice shapes how we serve our community.
