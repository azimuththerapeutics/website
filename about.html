About Waypoint

Our story, our values, and the people who make collaborative care possible.

Our Mission

Waypoint Cooperative is dedicated to providing accessible, high-quality mental health care rooted in collaboration, community ownership, and evidence-based practice. We believe that mental health services are strengthened when the people who deliver them are invested partners in a shared vision—not just employees. Through our worker-owned cooperative model, we create a practice where clinician wellbeing directly supports client wellbeing, and where every person on our team has a voice in our decisions.

Our Core Values

🤝

Collaboration

We believe the best care emerges from providers working together—sharing cases, supporting each other, and coordinating treatment. Your care benefits from multiple perspectives and collective expertise.

🌍

Community

As a cooperative, we're invested in our local community. We offer sliding scale fees, donate to local mental health initiatives, and believe mental health care is a community resource, not just a commodity.

📈

Growth & Learning

We're committed to ongoing professional development. Our team regularly pursues continuing education, training, and skill development—ensuring you receive care informed by the latest evidence and best practices.

🔓

Accessibility

Mental health care should be available to everyone. We accept insurance, offer self-pay options, and maintain a sliding scale fee structure so financial barriers don't prevent you from getting the support you deserve.

Why a Cooperative?

You've likely worked with mental health practices run as traditional businesses. Waypoint is different—and intentionally so.

Clinician Wellbeing = Better Care

In traditional practices, clinicians often report feeling undervalued and burned out. We've structured Waypoint differently. As worker-owners, our team members are invested in the practice's long-term health and sustainability. That means fair compensation, a voice in decision-making, and genuine peer support—not office politics.

Your Care Comes First

Because we're not answerable to outside investors or shareholders, we can prioritize clinical quality and client outcomes. We can say "no" to practices that might harm your care, and "yes" to innovations that serve your wellbeing.

Transparency & Trust

You'll find our team genuinely committed to their work. There's no hidden agenda, no pressure to over-bill insurance or extend treatment unnecessarily. We're here for your mental health, period.

Reinvestment in Care

Profits stay in the practice, funding better training, more group programs, community outreach, and sliding scale capacity. Your care directly supports the growth of a community resource.

"A cooperative model in mental health care is rare—and transformative. It means clinicians are truly partners, not employees. That changes everything about how we work together and how we show up for you."

— Erin O'Neil, LCSW, CEO & Founder
