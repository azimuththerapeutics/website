New Client Information

Here's what to expect when you reach out to Waypoint Cooperative.

1

Initial Contact

Call us at (860) 309-5980 or send an email to hello@waypointcoop.org. Tell us a bit about what brings you to therapy and we'll help match you with an appropriate provider.

2

Insurance Verification

If you have insurance, we'll verify your coverage and benefits before your first appointment. We'll explain your copay, coinsurance, and deductible so you know what to expect.

3

Scheduling

We'll offer appointment times that work with your schedule. We offer in-person sessions at our Torrington office and telehealth appointments via secure video.

4

First Appointment

Your initial session is an intake appointment. Your therapist will ask about your history, current concerns, goals, and preferences. This is also your chance to ask questions about treatment.

5

Ongoing Care

You'll typically schedule follow-up appointments weekly or as recommended by your therapist. We're flexible and can adjust frequency as your needs change.

6

Collaboration

If you're seeing multiple providers (e.g., therapist + psychiatrist), we coordinate care and communicate to ensure your treatment is integrated and effective.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does therapy cost?

Costs vary based on provider, modality (individual vs. group), and whether you use insurance, self-pay, or sliding scale. We accept most major insurances. Learn more about billing.

How long does therapy usually take?

This varies widely. Some people benefit from brief, focused therapy (6-12 weeks), while others work with the same therapist long-term. Your therapist will discuss treatment duration with you.

Do you accept my insurance?

We're in-network with most major insurers. Contact us with your insurance information and we'll verify your coverage before your first appointment.

Can I do therapy via video (telehealth)?

Yes! All our providers offer telehealth sessions via secure video. You can attend from home or anywhere private with internet access.

What if I'm in crisis or having suicidal thoughts?

If you're in immediate danger, please call 911 or go to your nearest emergency room. For crisis support, contact the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (call or text 988) or text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line).

Are you currently accepting new clients?

We are generally accepting new clients. Contact us to discuss current availability. We work hard to accommodate new client requests.

Can teens come without parental consent?

In Connecticut, youth 16+ can access mental health services without parental consent. For younger clients, parental involvement is part of standard care.

What makes your cooperative model different?

As a worker-owned cooperative, we're invested in both client care and clinician wellbeing. There's no external pressure to over-bill or extend treatment—just collaborative, ethical practice. Learn more about our cooperative model.

How do I prepare for my first session?

Come as you are! You might bring your insurance card and ID. Your therapist will guide you through intake forms before the appointment. Just be open and honest—your therapist is here to help, not judge.

What if I don't feel like the provider is the right fit?

We want you to feel safe and supported. If you feel mismatched with your provider, tell us. We're happy to discuss a referral to another clinician.

Crisis & Emergency Resources
