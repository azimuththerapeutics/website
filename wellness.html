Wellness

Beyond medication, whole-person care

Psychiatric treatment extends beyond prescriptions. Azimuth integrates evidence-based supportive services into your care — movement, mindfulness, and bodywork as therapeutic complements.

Supportive Services

Movement, mindfulness, and bodywork as therapeutic complements — not replacements — to psychiatric care.

🧘

Yoga Instruction

In-House & Outside Providers

As a 200-hour trained yoga teacher, AJ integrates yoga instruction, breathwork, and somatic awareness directly into treatment. Yoga supports nervous system regulation, emotional processing, and embodied mindfulness — offering a bridge between the clinical session and daily life. Additional instruction available through partnered yoga studios.

📍

Acupuncture

Professional Agreements with Outside Providers

Acupuncture has demonstrated efficacy for anxiety, insomnia, chronic pain, and stress-related conditions. Azimuth maintains professional agreements with licensed acupuncturists who understand the intersection of traditional Chinese medicine and psychiatric care, ensuring coordinated treatment aligned with your goals.

🤲

Bodywork & Massage

Professional Agreements with Outside Providers

Trauma and chronic stress live in the body. Therapeutic bodywork — including massage, myofascial release, and craniosacral therapy — helps release physical tension patterns associated with anxiety, PTSD, and chronic pain. Coordinated through professional agreements with licensed bodywork practitioners.

Experiential Therapy

Healing doesn't only happen in an office. The outdoors offers a powerful therapeutic environment — reducing cortisol, improving mood, and building resilience through direct experience. Azimuth integrates curated outdoor activities into treatment plans as a complement to traditional psychiatric care.

Nature-Based Regulation

Trail running, hiking, and gravel cycling engage the body's natural stress response systems. Movement in nature activates parasympathetic pathways that medication alone cannot reach — building lasting capacity for emotional regulation.

Challenge & Mastery

Ice climbing, skiing, and paddling demand present-moment awareness and progressive skill building. These experiences create concrete evidence of competence that directly counters the cognitive distortions of depression and anxiety.

Social Connection

Shared outdoor experiences — group rides, fly fishing, camping — build authentic social bonds outside clinical settings. Connection forged through shared challenge is qualitatively different from what a therapy group can offer.
